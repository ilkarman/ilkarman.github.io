Ilia Karmanov

Research Scientist at NVIDIA · Zurich

I studied economics at LSE (BSc and MSc), taught myself machine learning, and have since worked at Microsoft, Qualcomm AI Research, and NVIDIA.

At NVIDIA, I work on document understanding and vision-language models. I first-authored Eclair, a document digitization model used in the Nemotron-H pre-training pipeline, and have contributed to Eagle 2, Nemotron Nano V2 VL, and Nemotron Parse (deployed with open weights).

At Qualcomm AI Research (2020–2022), I worked on 3D computer vision and efficient architectures, publishing at NeurIPS (with Max Welling), ICCV, and BMVC, and filing 12 patent applications. At Microsoft (2016–2020), I worked on applied ML and initiated an open-source DL benchmarking project (1,700+ stars).

Before ML, my MSc thesis used optimal control theory to model corporate behaviour under reputational incentives. I then worked as a research economist at an Oxford research centre (directed by Prof. Paul Collier), and as a research assistant to Prof. Frank Cowell at LSE on causal inference work that led to a published paper.

Research interests: multimodal reasoning, document understanding, efficient architectures, scalable training.
