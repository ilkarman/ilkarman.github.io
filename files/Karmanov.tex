\section{Overview}
\small{
Senior Staff Research Scientist at NVIDIA ADLR, working on post-training and evaluation of vision-language models for multimodal reasoning. First author of Eclair, a document-understanding model adopted across NVIDIA's pipelines for pre-training data extraction and VLM distillation, and shipped as Nemotron Parse. Earlier, published at NeurIPS, ICCV, and BMVC at Qualcomm AI Research. Trained in economics at LSE (BSc First Class; MSc), with a focus on optimal control and causal inference, before moving into machine learning in 2016.
}

    \cventry
      {NVIDIA ADLR, Zurich}{Sep 2022 -- Feb 2025}
      {Staff Research Scientist}{}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item First author of Eclair, a document-understanding model that extracts formatted text, tables, formulas, and reading order. Adopted across NVIDIA's pipelines for pre-training data extraction (50B+ tokens) and as a teacher for VLM distillation; shipped with open weights as Nemotron Parse
        \item Led the model's design: an asymmetric architecture (larger vision encoder, lightweight decoder), no positional embeddings in the decoder, multi-token prediction to reduce decoding steps, and a single prompt-controlled output format spanning text, layout, and semantic classes
        \item Diagnosed and fixed a data-conditioning failure where the model keyed on visual appearance rather than the text prompt, using a self-correcting labelling method (patented)
        \item Contributed to the token-compression design for dense OCR in Eagle 2 (NeurIPS 2025). Mentored two interns (both later joined full-time)
      \end{itemize}
